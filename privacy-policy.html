Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Introduction

Touchstone Electrical Services ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website and use our services.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our site or services.

Application of This Policy

As a business with annual turnover under $3 million, Touchstone Electrical Services is generally exempt from most obligations under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). However, we have chosen to handle your personal information consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) as a matter of good practice and professional responsibility.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

When you submit an enquiry form, request a quote, or contact us by phone or email, we collect the following information:

  • Your name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Postal address and postcode
  • Details about your electrical service needs
  • Any additional information you provide in messages or comments

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you browse our website, we collect limited information automatically, including:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages you visit and time spent on each page
  • Referring website URL
  • Device type (mobile, desktop, tablet)

This information is collected via Google Analytics to help us understand how visitors use our website and improve our services.

2. Use of Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To respond to your enquiry, provide quotes, and deliver electrical services
  • Communication: To contact you about your request, schedule appointments, or provide updates
  • Business Operations: To manage our business, including invoicing, payment processing, and warranty tracking
  • Marketing: To send you occasional newsletters, service updates, or promotional offers (only with your consent)
  • Improvement: To improve our website, services, and customer experience
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with laws, regulations, and contractual obligations

3. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal information on the following bases:

  • Contract Performance: Information necessary to provide electrical services you've requested
  • Consent: For marketing communications, we only send these with your express consent
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with taxation, safety, and other regulatory requirements
  • Legitimate Interests: To improve services and protect our business

4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell or rent your personal information. We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

4.1 Service Providers

We may share information with third parties who assist us in delivering services, including:

  • Google Workspace (Gmail) for email communication
  • Xero (accounting software) for invoicing and payment processing
  • Google Analytics for website analytics
  • Insurance and warranty providers (for job documentation)

All service providers are bound by confidentiality agreements and may only use your information for the purposes we specify.

4.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or government agency request.

4.3 Business Transfer

If our business is sold, merged, or acquired, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change and any choices you may have.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide services and fulfill contractual obligations
  • Comply with legal and tax requirements (Australian Taxation Office: 5 years for most records; Fair Work: 7 years for employment and payroll records)
  • Maintain warranty and service records
  • Respond to disputes or claims

After this period, we securely delete or anonymize your information. If you request deletion of your data, we will comply unless we are legally required to retain it.

6. Security of Your Information

We take the security of your personal information seriously. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data, including:

  • Secure contact forms with encryption
  • Password-protected business systems
  • Limited employee access to personal information
  • Regular security audits and updates

However, no security system is impenetrable. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for keeping your passwords and account details confidential.

6.1 Data Breach Notification

If we become aware of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm, we will investigate promptly and, where required, notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) in line with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth).

7. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses the following tracking technologies:

  • Google Analytics: Collects anonymous data about how you use our website (page views, session duration, device type). You can opt out by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Essential Cookies: Small files that store your preferences and help the website function properly. These do not track personal behavior.

We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking pixels for marketing purposes. You can disable non-essential cookies through your browser settings, though this may limit some functionality of our website.

8. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (e.g., Google Maps, social media). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. Please review their privacy policies before providing any information.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Although Touchstone Electrical Services is generally exempt from the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), we voluntarily offer the following rights:

9.1 Access Your Information

You can request access to the personal information we hold about you. We will provide this information within 30 days of your request.

9.2 Correct Your Information

You can request corrections to any inaccurate or incomplete information we hold.

9.3 Opt Out of Marketing

You can unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time by clicking the "Unsubscribe" link in any communication or contacting us directly.

9.4 Request Deletion

You can request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements.

9.5 Making a Complaint

If you believe we have mishandled your personal information, please contact us first using the details in Section 13. We will investigate and respond within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you can lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.

10. Children's Privacy

Our services and website are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that a child has provided us with information without the consent of a parent or guardian, we will delete it promptly and take appropriate steps.

11. International Data Transfers

Some of the third-party service providers we use for email (Google Workspace), accounting (Xero), analytics (Google Analytics), and website hosting may store or process information on servers located overseas, including in the United States and other countries. These providers have their own privacy policies. We take reasonable steps to ensure overseas recipients handle your information consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles.

We will notify you of material changes by posting the updated policy on our website with a new "Last Updated" date. Your continued use of our services constitutes acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.
